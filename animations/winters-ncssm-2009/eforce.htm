Dr. Winters,

I have modified the Electrical Force applet. The "Mode" input has the following features:

Mode -1 gives strictly horizontal motion. Initial Y pos is set to zero, but initial X pos may be modified.

Mode 1 gives strictly vertical motion. Initial X pos is set to zero, but initial Y pos may be modified.

Mode 0 gives elliptical motion. Both initial Y pos and initial X pos may be modified. If the distance between the initial position and the origin is equal to the radius (such as if the initial position is (r,0)), then the ellipse will be a circle.

Mode 5 gives figure-eight motion. Both initial Y pos and initial X pos may be modified. It is suggested, but not necessary, that the initial position be (0,0).

Note that under no circumstances should the initial position be further away from the origin than the length of the radius. For example, an initial position of (2*r, r+1) would not function properly.

I have also added the floating text readout of the magnitude of the net force vector, as well as outputs giving the current X and Y position of the test charge.

I am sending this applet from my gmail account in the unlikely event that the filters also grab sent mail as well as received mail. I have modified the gas compression applet to allow for more inputs and more control of the animation by the teacher. However, I do not advise throwing out the older version, because that version had the distinction of calculating the physics for each of the three possible combinations of isobaric, isothermal, and adiabatic processes. In this new version, temperature and pressure will move between given initial and final values regardless of whether the values given actually make any sense. Thus, it is up to the creator of the applet to ensure that the values are realistic.

The relevant inputs are:

pit --- initial pressure
pif --- final pressure
tempi --- initial temperature
tempf --- final temperature
p_l_def -- lower bound of the pressure gauge
p_u_def --- upper bound of the pressure gause
t_l_def --- lower bound of the thermometer
t_u_def --- upper bound of the thermometer
